Go, Dog. Go!

P. D. Eastman

Go, Dog. Go! tells the story of a multitude of dogs moving about in varying modalities. In simple language, P.D. Eastman describes different facets of normal human life – work, play, sleep, and socialization (the story concludes with a big dog party).

A dialogue reoccurs between two dogs in which one dog asks the other dog, “Do you like my hat?” The two dogs meet in a number of scenarios and settings – scootering, skiing, and at a party. The motif illustrates plot, character, and relationship development. In each meeting, the hats become more extravagant.

In the first few instances, the responding dog does not like the hat-wearing dog’s hat. When asked at the dog party, “Do you like my hat?” the responding dog exclaims:

I do.
What a hat!
I like it!
I like that party hat!

The story is often misattributed as a Dr. Seuss creation. While he did not write the story, he did edit the book. P.D. Eastman wrote and illustrated other classics such as: Are You My Mother?; Red, Stop! Green, Go!; and Big Dog…Little Dog.
